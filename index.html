Monday
PUSH
Chest · Shoulders · Triceps · Core
Primary stimulus: horizontal and vertical push strength. Build chest width and shoulder mass — the visual foundation of the Fight Club silhouette.
Chest
Barbell Bench PressChest · Triceps · Front Delt
4 × 6–8▼

The primary strength builder. Heavy compound lift that overloads the entire pushing chain. Low-rep range builds dense, functional muscle — not puffy bulk. This is the cornerstone of the Monday session.

Incline DB PressUpper Chest · Front Delt
3 × 8–10▼

Targets the upper pec that creates the chest-to-shoulder shelf. Dumbbells allow greater range of motion and correct side-to-side imbalances the barbell masks.

Cable FlyChest (stretch focus)
3 × 12–15▼

Isolates the pec under constant cable tension throughout the full arc. Higher reps drive blood flow and metabolic stress — the pump that reinforces muscle-mind connection.

Shoulders
Seated Shoulder PressAll 3 Delt Heads · Triceps
3 × 8–10▼

Vertical compound lift for overhead shoulder strength. Seated removes lower-body momentum, forcing true shoulder drive. Critical for building the wide-shoulder silhouette.

Lateral RaisesLateral (Side) Delt
4 × 15–20▼

The single most impactful exercise for shoulder width. The lateral head only grows with direct isolation. High volume and high reps are essential — this muscle responds to accumulation, not heavy weight.

Triceps
Triceps Rope PushdownTriceps (all heads)
3 × 12–15▼

Isolation finisher after the triceps have been pre-fatigued by pressing. The rope allows hands to separate at the bottom, maximising the contraction. Keeps arms defined and tight.

Core
Hanging Leg RaiseLower Abs · Hip Flexors
3 × 10–15▼

Builds the lower abdominal wall — the hardest section to develop. Hanging also decompresses the spine after heavy pressing. No swinging — controlled descent is everything.

PlankDeep Core · Glutes · Shoulders
3 × 45–60s▼

Anti-extension core stability. Builds structural tension in the midsection that keeps the waist tight and supports all compound lifts. Not glamorous — structural armor.

!Rest 90–120s between compound sets. Rest 60s between isolation sets. Strength on the compounds, accumulation on the isolations — don't conflate the two.
Tuesday
PULL
Back · Biceps · Rear Delt · Core
Primary stimulus: vertical and horizontal pulling strength. Build the V-taper back and thick biceps that define the Fight Club upper body from the rear.
Back
Pull-ups / Lat PulldownLats · Biceps · Rear Delt
4 × 6–8▼

The primary vertical pull. Pull-ups are the gold standard. Use lat pulldown only when pull-ups can't be completed with good form. Width of the lats — and the V-taper — comes from here.

Barbell RowMid-Back · Lats · Rear Delt
4 × 6–8▼

The horizontal counterpart to the bench press. Builds thickness through the mid-back and rhomboids — the muscle that makes the back look 3D and carved. Full retraction at the top every rep.

One-Arm DB RowLats · Mid-Back
3 × 10–12▼

Unilateral rowing with greater range of motion than the barbell. Each side trained independently catches asymmetries. Pull the elbow past the hip for full lat engagement.

Face PullsRear Delt · Rotator Cuff · Traps
3 × 15–20▼

Non-negotiable for shoulder health. Counteracts the internal rotation built by heavy bench pressing. Keeps the shoulder joint stable and adds rear delt roundness visible from the back.

Biceps
Barbell CurlBiceps (long + short head)
3 × 8–10▼

The primary bicep mass builder. Controlled negative (3 seconds down) maximises mechanical tension — that's where the growth stimulus lives. Don't swing the weight.

Hammer CurlBrachialis · Brachioradialis
2 × 12▼

Neutral grip hits the brachialis beneath the bicep, pushing it up and creating arm thickness. Also builds forearm size — arms that look strong from every angle.

Core
Cable CrunchUpper Abs · Rectus Abdominis
3 × 12–15▼

One of the only weighted ab exercises that allows progressive overload. The cable maintains tension through the full arc — unlike floor crunches which go slack at the top.

Bicycle CrunchObliques · Upper Abs
3 × 20▼

Activates the obliques through rotation — the muscle that creates waist definition and the diagonal lines of a shredded midsection. Slow and deliberate, not a speed exercise.

!On pull-ups: dead hang at the bottom, chest to the bar at the top. If you can do more than 8 reps clean, add a weight belt. Strength on the compound pulls is the #1 driver of back development.
Wednesday
REST + WALK
Active Recovery · Fat Loss
No intensity today. The walk is a deliberate tool — not a warmup. LISS at this specification burns fat without elevating cortisol or competing with muscle recovery.
INCLINE TREADMILL WALK
Duration
30–45 min
Speed
5.5–6.5 km/h
Incline
6–10%
Target HR
120–135 bpm
Zone
Zone 2 (LISS)
The incline recruits more glute and hamstring than flat walking. Staying in Zone 2 means you're burning predominantly fat as fuel. Going above 135 bpm shifts to glycolytic energy and competes with recovery. Don't run. Sustained, low-stress output is the point.
!Use the walk productively — podcast, language lesson, or decompress. Avoid this within 2 hours of your heaviest meal for maximum fat oxidation.
Thursday
LEGS + CORE
Quads · Hamstrings · Glutes · Calves · Core
The most demanding session of the week. Leg training releases the most anabolic hormones of any session — it makes your whole body grow, not just your legs. Don't skip it.
Quads & Glutes
Back SquatQuads · Glutes · Core
4 × 6–8▼

The king of all lifts. Builds raw lower-body mass and drives systemic anabolic hormone output. Heavy compound squatting is one of the strongest signals for whole-body growth. Depth matters — below parallel.

Romanian DeadliftHamstrings · Glutes · Lower Back
3 × 8–10▼

Hip-hinge with a controlled stretch on the hamstrings at the bottom. Builds the dense hamstring sweep that balances quad-dominant squat work. Feel the stretch — don't chase the weight.

Leg PressQuads · Glutes
3 × 10–12▼

Higher quad volume after squats without taxing the lower back. Foot position controls emphasis — higher = more glute, lower = more quad. Never lock out hard at the top.

Hamstrings & Calves
Leg CurlHamstrings (isolated)
3 × 10–12▼

Trains the hamstring in knee flexion — a function the RDL alone doesn't cover. Both hip extension and knee flexion must be trained for complete hamstring development and knee health.

Standing Calf RaiseGastrocnemius · Soleus
4 × 15–20▼

Calves are mostly slow-twitch — they respond to high reps and frequency. Standing position emphasises the gastrocnemius (the visible "diamond" calf). Pause at the bottom stretch for 1 second each rep.

Core
Hanging Leg RaiseLower Abs · Hip Flexors
4 × 10–15▼

Four sets here vs three on Monday — Thursday is the primary core day. Full control on the way down. Momentum is wasted work.

PlankDeep Core · Glutes
3 × 45–60s▼

Reinforce the stability needed for heavy squats and deadlifts. After a hard leg session, this also serves as a focused breathing reset before leaving the gym.

Side Plank (each side)Obliques · Lateral Core
2 × 30s▼

Anti-lateral-flexion stability. Targets the obliques and quadratus lumborum — muscles that frame the waist and protect the spine under load.

!Eat a real meal 90–120 minutes before this session. Squats on empty is how form breaks and injuries happen. This is your hardest session of the week — treat it that way.

Saturday
METCON
Full Body · Conditioning · Lactic Threshold
Metabolic conditioning. Combines strength and high-output work to elevate EPOC — accelerating fat burn for 24–48 hours post-session. This is what cuts the physique.
Strength Complex
Military PressShoulders · Triceps · Upper Back
3 × 8▼

Strict overhead press — no leg drive. Reinforces shoulder strength from Monday. Done first while fresh, before the conditioning work degrades output.

DB Bent-Over RowBack · Biceps · Rear Delt
3 × 10▼

The pull counterpart to the press. Keeps the session balanced. Superset with military press for efficiency and heart rate elevation.

DipsLower Chest · Triceps · Front Delt
3 × 8▼

Bodyweight pushing compound — lower chest and deep triceps. Forward lean = more chest, upright = more triceps. A battle-tested indicator of true upper-body pushing strength.

Chin-UpsLats · Biceps · Rear Delt
3 × 8▼

Underhand grip shifts more load onto the biceps vs pull-ups. Paired with dips, creates a push/pull superset that maintains high metabolic stress through complementary movement patterns.

Shoulder Accessories
Lateral RaiseLateral Delt
3 × 15▼

Second weekly lateral delt dose. The side delt responds to frequency — twice per week accelerates width. Lighter here due to accumulated fatigue; focus on the burn, not the number on the dumbbell.

Face PullRear Delt · Rotator Cuff
3 × 15▼

Second weekly rear delt and rotator cuff dose. After a full week of pressing and pulling, external rotation work is critical. The maintenance check for your shoulder joints before the week resets.

Conditioning Finisher
Burpee CircuitFull Body · Cardiovascular
4 × 30s▼

30 seconds all-out, 20–30s rest, four rounds. Anaerobic threshold training — spikes heart rate into the red zone, creating the EPOC effect that burns calories for hours after the session ends. This is the metabolic cost that gets you lean.

Treadmill FinisherCardiovascular · Cooldown
10 min▼

Transition from anaerobic to aerobic. First 5 minutes at moderate pace (7–8 km/h), last 5 minutes walking. Prevents blood pooling and accelerates lactate clearance. Never stop cold after burpees.

!On burpees: quality over speed. A controlled, full-extension burpee with a real jump is worth 3× the sloppy half-reps. Let the 30 seconds do the work.
Sunday
FULL REST
Recovery · Sleep · Adaptation
Muscle is not built in the gym — it is built during recovery. Sunday is where the week's work becomes permanent adaptation. Protect it.
RECOVERY PROTOCOL
Sleep
7–8 hrs
Stretching
Optional · 10 min
Intensity
Zero
Protein target
1.8–2.2g / kg
Sleep is the most powerful recovery tool available — no supplement competes. During deep sleep, growth hormone peaks and muscle protein synthesis runs at full capacity. 7–8 hours is non-negotiable at this training volume. Eat well, hydrate, and let the adaptation happen.
!Review the week: Did you hit protein targets? Sleep well? Was there a session where form broke down? Small weekly adjustments compound into big results over time.
